# Data-Driven Market-Making via Model-Free Learning

Background:

  1. Modern US equity markets: electronic exchanges (~70%)

  2. Limit Order: buy and sell (at a specified price - bid, ask)

  3. Limit Order Book: a record of outstanding limit orders

Challenges:

  1. difficulty in estimating the transition probabilities of MDP

    • unpredictable market order arrivals
    • limit order arrivals and cancellations from other participants

  2. a very large state space (the notorious curse of dimensionality)

1 / 3

# Motivation

  • they take the perspective of a market-making firm

  • to provide real-time guidance for how to manage the firm’s portfolio of limit buy and sell orders on the LOB

    • limited mismatch between the amounts bought and sold
    • sufficient high Sharpe ratio (>1: acceptable, >2: very good, >3: excellent)

  • They implement a model-free Q-learning algorithm together with state aggregation
